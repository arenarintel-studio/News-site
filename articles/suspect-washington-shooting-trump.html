Suspect in Washington dinner shooting charged with attempting to assassinate Trump

By Arenar Intel April 27, 2026 • 10:06 AM UTC
Source: Reuters

WASHINGTON — The man accused of opening fire at a Washington dinner attended by Donald Trump has been charged with attempting to assassinate the U.S. president, Reuters reports.

Cole Tomas Allen, 31, appeared in Washington federal court on Monday, two days after authorities said he launched an attack at the White House Correspondents' Association Dinner. He faces a life sentence if convicted.

Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche told reporters that investigators believe Allen targeted Trump and had referred to the president as a "traitor" in an email sent to relatives the night of the incident.

Allen, of Torrance, California, also faces charges of illegally transporting a firearm across state lines and discharging a firearm during a crime of violence. He was ordered held in custody until at least Thursday.

Prosecutors said Allen brought a 12-gauge pump-action shotgun and three knives to Washington. A court filing also stated he was armed with a semi-automatic handgun.

According to an FBI affidavit, Allen sent an email to family members referring to himself as the "Friendly Federal Assassin" and discussed plans to target senior Trump administration officials.

The shooting disrupted the annual black-tie gathering of journalists and politicians. Trump, who was set to deliver remarks, was rushed off stage by security personnel. A Secret Service officer was shot in the chest but was wearing a ballistic vest and survived.

Defense lawyer Tezira Abe said Allen had no prior arrests or convictions. He did not respond to the allegations at the hearing.
